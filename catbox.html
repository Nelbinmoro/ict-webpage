Lamborghini

Lamborghini is an Italian car manufacturer that produces sports cars. Ferruccio Lamborghini, an Italian manufacturing magnate, founded the company in 1963 in Sant'Agata Bolognese, Italy, with the goal of building a grand touring car to compete with Ferrari. The company's first models, including the 350 GT, were released in the mid-1960s.

Supra MK4

The Toyota Supra MK4 is a car that was produced between 1993 and 2002. It has a 3.0-liter straight-six engine with a cast-iron block and 7 main bearings, and can produce up to 320 horsepower and 440 Nm of torque. The base model has a 4-speed automatic transmission, but a 6-speed manual is also available

Ferrari

Ferrari is an Italian luxury sports car manufacturer based in Maranello, Italy. Enzo Ferrari, a former Alfa Romeo salesman and racing driver, founded the company in 1929 as Scuderia Ferrari, a Grand Prix racing team. The company adopted its current name in 1945 and has been involved in motorsport ever since, competing in Formula One and sports car racing.

Skyline R34

The Nissan Skyline GT-R R34 is a four-seat, two-door coupe that was produced from 1999 to 2002. It's the fifth and final generation of the Skyline GT-R, and is considered one of the most iconic Japanese sports cars of all time. The R34 GT-R has a 2.6-liter, twin-turbocharged, inline-six engine that produces 280 horsepower, and a six-speed manual transmission.

BMW M4

The BMW M4 is a high-performance coupe with a 6-cylinder gasoline engine and rear-wheel drive. It's known for its power, handling, and sporty styling. Reviews say it's exceptional to drive, has plenty of power, and a spacious interior, but it has limited rear headroom and some say the looks are divisive.
